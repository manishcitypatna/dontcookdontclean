A close friend of mine moved to Bangalore for work a few years ago. Like most working professionals juggling long hours, he decided to hire a full-time domestic helper — someone to cook, clean, and manage the house while he was at the office.
 
He found a maid through a local contact, a woman from Nepal who seemed reliable enough during the initial conversation. Being cautious by nature, he did the one thing most people remember to do — he asked for her Aadhaar card before she started working. It felt like the responsible thing to do. A document on file, a name, an address. Surely that was enough.
 
For a while, everything was normal. She cooked, cleaned, kept the house in order. My friend even lent her a few personal items temporarily — nothing huge, but things he expected to get back eventually, the way you would with someone you've started to trust over weeks of working together.
 
Then one day, she simply didn't show up.
 
No call. No message. No explanation.
 
He waited a day, then two, growing more concerned not just about the missing help but about his belongings that were still with her. When he tried calling the number she had given him, it didn't go through. The number was either switched off permanently or had never been entirely genuine to begin with.
 
He had her Aadhaar card. That should have been enough to track her down, right?
 
It wasn't.
 
---
 
## Why a Single Document Often Isn't Enough
 
This is the part of the story that stuck with me the most. My friend had done more due diligence than most people bother to do — he had a copy of her ID before she even started working. And yet, when things went wrong, that single piece of paper didn't lead anywhere useful.
 
Here's the uncomfortable truth about hiring domestic help informally, whether in Bangalore, Patna, or anywhere else in India:
 
**An ID copy alone is not verification.** It's a starting point, but it doesn't confirm the address is current, doesn't confirm the phone number is genuinely hers, and doesn't tell you anything about her work history or whether she's done this before with someone else.
 
**There's no real accountability without a structured process.** When you hire through an informal contact — a neighbour's recommendation, a WhatsApp forward, a local broker — there's nobody to escalate to if something goes wrong. No agency, no record, no responsibility beyond the piece of paper you collected yourself.
 
**By the time something happens, it's often too late.** My friend didn't have a previous employer reference, a verified current address, or anyone vouching for her work history beyond the introduction itself. When she disappeared, there was simply nowhere left to look.

## What You Should Always Verify Before Hiring a Maid or Cook in Patna
 
Whether you work with us or hire independently, here is the minimum you should never skip:
 
1. **A government-issued photo ID** — Aadhaar card or equivalent, with a copy kept on file
2. **A current, verified phone number** — confirmed by calling it yourself before the helper starts, not just noting it down
3. **At least one previous employer reference** — call and ask directly about reliability and conduct
4. **A documented address** — ideally confirmed, not just stated
5. **A trial period** — a few days before committing to a full month, so you can observe behavior and build initial trust
None of these steps are complicated. They simply require someone to actually follow through on them, which is the part that gets skipped most often — exactly what happened with my friend in Bangalore.
