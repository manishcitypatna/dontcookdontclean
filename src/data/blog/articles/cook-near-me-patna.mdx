## What Type of Cook Do You Actually Need?

Before you search for a cook near you in Patna, be clear about what you need. This saves time and avoids mismatches.

### Part-Time Cook (1 to 2 Hours Daily)
Best for families who need help with one or two meals a day. The cook comes in the morning or evening, prepares the meal, and leaves. This is the most common arrangement in Patna.

### Half-Day Cook (4 to 5 Hours Daily)
Ideal if you need someone to handle breakfast, lunch prep, and kitchen cleaning. Good for households with school-going children or working parents.

### Full-Time Cook (8 to 10 Hours Daily)
Suited for larger families or households where cooking and kitchen management need full attention throughout the day.

### Live-In Cook
For families who need round-the-clock meal support — often preferred by elderly households, joint families, or families with specific dietary needs.

## How to Verify a Home Cook in Patna Before Hiring

Whether you find a cook through an agency or directly, always verify these before letting anyone into your home:

### 1. Government-Issued ID
Ask for Aadhaar card, voter ID, or any government photo ID. Note down the details and keep a photocopy.

### 2. Previous Employer Reference
Ask for at least one reference from a previous family they worked with in Patna. Call and confirm.

### 3. Address Verification
Make sure the cook lives in or near Patna. Someone commuting from a distant area may not show up reliably long-term.

### 4. Cooking Trial
Before finalising, ask for a one-day cooking trial. This tells you more than any interview — you will know immediately whether their cooking style matches your family's taste.

### 5. Written Agreement
Keep the arrangement documented — working hours, tasks, monthly salary, leave policy, and notice period. This protects both sides.

## What Makes a Good Home Cook — Beyond Just Cooking

When families in Patna hire a cook, they are not just hiring someone who can make dal and roti. They are bringing someone into their home daily. Here is what to look for beyond cooking skill:

**Punctuality** — A cook who arrives on time every day is worth more than one with great recipes but poor reliability.

**Hygiene habits** — Observe whether they wash hands before cooking, keep the kitchen clean while working, and store food properly.

**Adaptability** — Every family has different tastes, dietary restrictions, and schedules. A good cook adjusts without resistance.

**Communication** — They should be able to tell you when an ingredient is running low, when they need a day off in advance, and if there is any issue in the kitchen.
