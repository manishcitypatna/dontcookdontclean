## What Most Boring Road Residents Get Wrong When Hiring
 
### Relying Only on Society Guards or Neighbours
Asking a building guard or neighbour for a maid referral is extremely common in Boring Road, and it does work — many maids genuinely do work across multiple flats in the same building or nearby complexes. But this approach has a blind spot: you are trusting a second-hand recommendation with no documentation, no formal verification, and no recourse if something goes wrong.
 
### Not Clarifying Leave Policy Upfront
Most full-time and live-in arrangements in Patna include 1 to 2 leaves per month as standard. If this isn't discussed before the helper starts, it becomes a recurring point of friction.
 
### Assuming Festival Cleaning Is Included
Deep cleaning before Chhath, Diwali, or other festivals is commonly treated as extra work beyond the regular monthly salary in Boring Road households. Clarify this and agree on any additional payment in advance, not after the work is done.
 
### Skipping ID Verification Because "the Guard Knows Them"
Even with a guard's vouching, always ask for a copy of a government-issued photo ID and a passport-sized photograph yourself. If your apartment society requires documentation for gate passes — which many in Boring Road do — you will need this anyway.

## Society Gate Pass Requirements in Boring Road
 
Many apartment complexes along Boring Road, Patliputra Colony, and S.K. Puri require domestic staff to be registered with society management for gate access. If you are hiring someone new, be prepared to submit:
 
- A copy of their Aadhaar card or other photo ID
- A passport-sized photograph
- Basic contact details
Having this documentation ready in advance — rather than scrambling after hiring — makes the onboarding process smoother for both you and your new helper.
